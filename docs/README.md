Welcome to OctoAcme's centralized project management knowledge base. This README provides a quick overview of our project management processes and links to all detailed workflows and role definitions used across our organization.

Project Management Processes – Summary
OctoAcme standardizes how projects are initiated, planned, executed, released, and continually improved. Key principles: customer-first, clear ownership, iterative delivery, and psychological safety. Core roles include Project Manager, Product Manager, Developers, QA, and Stakeholders. We maintain clear communication, risk registers, and focus on data-informed decisions throughout the project lifecycle.

Lifecycle Stages:

Initiation – Validate business need, align stakeholders, define success criteria (see octoacme-project-initiation.md)
Planning – Backlog setup, estimation, Definition of Done, release planning (octoacme-project-planning.md)
Execution & Tracking – Standups, syncs, demos, QA/testing (octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)
Risk Management & Communication – Risk register, stakeholder updates, escalation (octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)
Release & Deployment – Release types, checklists, rollback playbook (octoacme-release-and-deployment.md)
Retrospective & Continuous Improvement – Learnings and action items (octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md)
Roles & Personas – Responsibilities and communication norms (octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)
